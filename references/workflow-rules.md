# Post-Publish Review Rules

Use these rules when running `$xhs-postpublish-review`.

## Inputs To Check

- note link or identifier
- title and cover
- script, caption, or page text
- backend screenshot or manually typed metrics
- publish time and checkpoint: 24h, 48h, or 7d
- content goal

## Workflow Rules

- Match data to the exact note.
- Judge exposure and click issues.
- Judge read, completion, or retention issues.
- Judge save, comment, share, and follow conversion.
- Connect the data back to title, cover, opening, body, and audience promise.
- Choose one next test.

## Output Must Include

- what the data most likely means
- where the note is leaking users
- what to change next time
- whether to remake title, cover, opening, body, or topic
- one next publishing experiment

## Quality Checks

- The output must be usable from the material provided by the user.
- Recommendations must explain their reasoning.
- If evidence is weak or missing, mark it clearly.
- Keep the first result focused; do not expand into a full consulting report by default.
- Do not claim automatic publishing, guaranteed growth, guaranteed traffic, or guaranteed sales.
- Do not copy another creator's exact wording, images, story, identity, or claims.

## Public-Safe Wording

Prefer:

- workflow
- checklist
- review
- judgment
- public signal
- user-provided material
- next action

Avoid:

- private data extraction
- secret system
- guaranteed viral result
- guaranteed traffic growth
- copy-paste another creator's content
